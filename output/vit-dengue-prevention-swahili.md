## 3. Tafsiri ya Kiswahili (maandishi makuu / primary deliverable)

### Ukweli muhimu

- Homa ya dengue ni maambukizi ya virusi yanayoenezwa kwa binadamu kupitia kuumwa na mbu
  walioambukizwa, hasa mbu wa aina ya **_Aedes aegypti_**, na kwa kiasi kidogo zaidi
  **_Aedes albopictus_**.
- Sehemu kubwa ya watu duniani wako hatarini, hasa katika maeneo ya tropiki na nusu-tropiki.
- Maambukizi mengi hayaonyeshi dalili au huwa hafifu, na watu wengi hupona ndani ya wiki 1–2.
- Baadhi ya wagonjwa hupatwa na hali **kali (homa ya dengue kali / *severe dengue*)** inayohitaji
  huduma ya hospitalini, na inaweza kuua.
- **Hakuna matibabu mahususi** ya homa ya dengue; lakini ugunduzi wa mapema na upatikanaji wa huduma
  ifaayo ya kitabibu hupunguza sana kiwango cha vifo.
- Kinga na udhibiti hutegemea kwa kiasi kikubwa **udhibiti wa vekta (mbu)**.

### Jinsi maambukizi yanavyoenea (usambazaji)

- Husababishwa na virusi vya dengue (DENV), vyenye aina nne za serotype (DENV-1 hadi DENV-4).
  Kupona kutoka aina moja hutoa kinga ya maisha dhidi ya aina hiyo mahususi, lakini kinga dhidi ya
  aina nyingine ni ya sehemu na ya muda mfupi; kwa hivyo kuambukizwa baadaye na aina tofauti
  **huongeza hatari ya kupatwa na dengue kali**.
- Virusi huenea hasa kupitia mbu **_Aedes aegypti_**. Mbu huyu huwa hai **mchana**, na kuuma kwake
  huongezeka asubuhi na mapema na kabla ya machweo.
- Mbu hawa huzaliana katika **makusanyiko madogo ya maji yaliyotuama** (vyombo vya nyumbani,
  matairi yaliyotumika, n.k.).
- Kwa kiasi kidogo, virusi vyaweza pia kuenea kupitia kuongezewa damu, upandikizaji wa viungo, na
  kutoka kwa mama kwenda kwa mtoto aliye tumboni.

### Ishara na dalili

- Watu wengi walioambukizwa hawaonyeshi dalili. Dalili zinapotokea, kwa kawaida huanza baada ya
  **kipindi cha kuatamia (incubation) cha siku 4–10** tangu kuumwa.
- Homa ya dengue hushukiwa wakati kuna **homa kali** ikiambatana na dalili mbili au zaidi kati ya
  zifuatazo:
  - maumivu makali ya kichwa;
  - maumivu nyuma ya macho;
  - maumivu ya misuli na viungo;
  - kichefuchefu au kutapika;
  - kuvimba kwa tezi;
  - vipele (upele) wa ngozi.
- Dalili kwa kawaida hudumu kwa siku 2–7, kisha wagonjwa wengi hupona.

### Dalili za onyo za dengue kali — dharura

Mara nyingi dalili za onyo hujitokeza **baada ya homa kupungua** (kwa kawaida kati ya siku ya 3 na
ya 7 tangu kuanza kwa ugonjwa). **Tafuta huduma ya kitabibu mara moja** ikiwa lolote kati ya
yafuatayo litatokea:

- maumivu makali ya tumbo;
- kutapika kusikokoma;
- kupumua kwa haraka;
- kutokwa damu kwenye fizi au pua;
- uchovu mkali au kukosa utulivu;
- damu katika matapishi au kinyesi;
- kiu kikali;
- ngozi iliyopauka na yenye baridi;
- hisia ya udhaifu mkubwa.

> **Dengue kali ni dharura ya kitabibu inayohatarisha maisha na yaweza kuua. Usisubiri — nenda
> kituo cha afya kilicho karibu zaidi mara moja.**

### Huduma na matibabu

- **Hakuna matibabu mahususi ya kuua virusi (antiviral).** Huduma hulenga kupunguza dalili na
  kumsaidia mgonjwa.
- Inafaa **kupumzika** na **kunywa maji ya kutosha**.
- **Parasetamoli (asetaminofeni)** yaweza kutumika kushusha homa na kutuliza maumivu.
- **Epuka** dawa za kuzuia uvimbe zisizo za steroidi (NSAIDs) kama vile **ibuprofeni** na
  **aspirini**, kwa sababu zaweza kuongeza hatari ya kuvuja damu.
- Fuatilia kuonekana kwa dalili za onyo, na tafuta huduma ya kitabibu mara moja ikiwa lolote
  litatokea.

> ⚠️ Dokezo kwa mkaguzi: Sehemu inayohusu dawa na kuepuka NSAIDs ni sehemu **nyeti kwa usalama**;
> uandishi wake lazima uthibitishwe kwa makini (ona §6 na §7).

### Chanjo

- Chanjo dhidi ya homa ya dengue zipo, lakini mapendekezo kuhusu matumizi yake na makundi
  yanayostahili **hubadilika na hutofautiana kati ya nchi na nchi**.
- Fuata daima **mwongozo wa sasa wa mamlaka ya afya ya taifa lako na wa WHO** kuhusu chanjo. Chanjo
  haichukui nafasi ya udhibiti wa mbu na kinga binafsi.

### Kinga na udhibiti (sehemu kuu)

Njia bora ya kujikinga na homa ya dengue ni **kuepuka kuumwa na mbu** na **kuondoa mazalia yao**.
Kwa kuwa mbu msambazaji huuma mchana, ulinzi unahitajika hata wakati wa mchana.

**Kwanza: Kinga binafsi dhidi ya kuumwa**
- Vaa mavazi yanayofunika sehemu kubwa ya mwili iwezekanavyo.
- Tumia **vyandarua** wakati wa kulala mchana, ikiwezekana vile **vilivyotiwa dawa ya kuua wadudu**,
  hasa kuwalinda watoto wachanga, watoto wadogo, wajawazito, na wagonjwa.
- Weka **nyavu za waya** kwenye madirisha na milango.
- Tumia **dawa za kufukuza mbu (repellents)** kwenye ngozi iliyo wazi au nguo (mfano, zile zenye
  **DEET**, **picaridin**, au **IR3535**), ukifuata maelekezo ya kibandiko kwa makini.
- **Mizunguko ya kufukuza mbu (coils)** na **vinukizo** yaweza kutumika ndani ya nyumba.

**Pili (muhimu zaidi): Kuondoa mazalia ya mbu**
- **Funika** vyombo vya kuhifadhi maji vya nyumbani, na **vimwage na kuvisafisha kila wiki**.
- Ondoa au pindua vitu vyovyote vinavyokusanya maji yaliyotuama (matairi yaliyotumika, ndoo,
  vyungu, sahani za vyungu vya mimea).
- Tupa taka ngumu ipasavyo ili maji yasikusanyike ndani yake.
- **Dawa za kuua viluwiluwi (larvicides)** zinazofaa zaweza kuongezwa kwenye matangi ya maji ya
  nje yasiyoweza kufunikwa.

**Tatu: Hatua za kijamii**
- Shiriki katika **kampeni za usafi** na uhamasishaji wa jamii ili kuondoa mazalia ya mbu.
- Wakati wa milipuko, hatua za dharura zaweza kujumuisha **kunyunyiza dawa za kuua wadudu angani
  (space spraying)** ili kupunguza idadi ya mbu waliokomaa.
- Saidia **ufuatiliaji na ukaguzi wa mbu** kwa ajili ya ugunduzi wa mapema wa hatari.

> **Lini kutafuta huduma:** Kila mtu mwenye dalili za onyo za dengue kali lazima atafute huduma ya
> kitabibu **mara moja**. Na pale homa ya dengue inapoenea katika eneo lako, nenda kituo cha afya
> mapema ukipatwa na homa.

## 4. English source-equivalent text (for reviewer verification)

> Provided so the reviewer can verify the Swahili against the source. ⚠️ **This English text must be
> reconciled against the live WHO fact sheet before sign-off** — it reflects established WHO dengue
> guidance but was not captured by live fetch.

**Key facts.** Dengue is a viral infection spread to humans through the bite of infected mosquitoes,
chiefly *Aedes aegypti* (and, to a lesser extent, *Aedes albopictus*). A large share of the world's
population is at risk, especially in tropical and subtropical areas. Many infections are
asymptomatic or mild and most people recover in 1–2 weeks. Some develop **severe dengue**, which
needs hospital care and can be fatal. **There is no specific treatment**; early detection and access
to proper medical care greatly lower fatality. Prevention and control depend mainly on **mosquito
(vector) control**.

**Transmission.** Caused by dengue virus (DENV), with four serotypes (DENV-1 to DENV-4). Recovery
from one serotype gives lifelong immunity to that serotype but only partial, temporary protection
against the others; a later infection with a different serotype **increases the risk of severe
dengue**. Spread mainly by *Aedes aegypti*, which **bites during the daytime** (peaking in early
morning and before dusk). These mosquitoes breed in **small collections of standing water**
(household containers, used tires, etc.). Less commonly, the virus can spread via blood transfusion,
organ transplant, and from mother to fetus.

**Signs and symptoms.** Most people are asymptomatic. When symptoms occur, they appear after a
**4–10 day incubation** and include **high fever** plus two or more of: severe headache, pain behind
the eyes, muscle and joint pains, nausea, vomiting, swollen glands, rash. Symptoms usually last 2–7
days.

**Severe dengue — warning signs (emergency).** Often appear **after the fever subsides** (typically
days 3–7 of illness). **Seek care immediately** for: severe abdominal pain; persistent vomiting;
rapid breathing; bleeding gums or nose; severe fatigue or restlessness; blood in vomit or stool;
extreme thirst; pale, cold skin; weakness. **Severe dengue is a life-threatening medical
emergency — go to the nearest health facility at once.**

**Care and treatment.** No specific antiviral treatment; care is supportive — **rest** and adequate
**fluids**. **Paracetamol (acetaminophen)** may be used for fever and pain. **Avoid NSAIDs such as
ibuprofen and aspirin**, which can increase bleeding risk. Watch for warning signs and seek care
immediately if any appear.

**Vaccines.** Dengue vaccines exist, but recommendations on their use and eligible groups **vary by
country and change over time**. Always follow current **national health authority and WHO**
guidance. Vaccination does not replace mosquito control and personal protection.

**Prevention and control.** The best prevention is to **avoid mosquito bites** and **eliminate
breeding sites**; because the vector bites in daytime, protection is needed during the day.
*Personal protection:* wear covering clothing; use mosquito nets (ideally insecticide-treated),
especially for infants, young children, pregnant people, and the sick, when sleeping by day; fit
window/door screens; apply repellents (e.g., DEET, picaridin, IR3535) to exposed skin/clothing per
label; use coils and vaporizers indoors. *Eliminate breeding sites (most important):* cover, empty,
and clean water-storage containers **weekly**; remove or overturn items that collect standing water
(used tires, buckets, pots, plant-pot saucers); dispose of solid waste properly; apply appropriate
**larvicides** to outdoor water containers that cannot be covered. *Community measures:* join
clean-up and awareness campaigns; during outbreaks, emergency control may include **space spraying**
of insecticides; support mosquito surveillance. *When to seek care:* anyone with severe-dengue
warning signs must seek care **immediately**.
