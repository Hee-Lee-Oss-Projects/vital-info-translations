### ዋና ዋና እውነታዎች / Key facts
- ዴንጊ (Dengue) በ*Aedes* ዝርያ — በተለይም *Aedes aegypti* — በተባለ የትንኝ ንክሻ የሚተላለፍ የቫይረስ በሽታ ነው።
- በሽታው በሞቃታማና እርጥበት አዘል አካባቢዎች በብዛት ይከሰታል። በሰው ለሰው በቀጥታ ንክኪ **አይተላለፍም**።
- አብዛኞቹ የተያዙ ሰዎች ምንም ምልክት አያሳዩም ወይም ቀላል ምልክት ብቻ ይኖራቸዋል፤ ብዙዎቹም በ1–2 ሳምንታት ውስጥ ይያገግማሉ።
- አልፎ አልፎ ግን ወደ **ጽኑ ዴንጊ (severe dengue)** ሊያድግ ይችላል፤ ይህ ለሕይወት አስጊ የሕክምና ድንገተኛ ሁኔታ ነው።
- ለዴንጊ የተለየ ፀረ-ቫይረስ መድኃኒት የለም። ሆኖም የአደጋ ምልክቶችን ቀድሞ ማወቅና ተገቢ የሕክምና እንክብካቤ ማግኘት የሞት እድልን በእጅጉ ይቀንሳል።

### ዴንጊ እንዴት ይተላለፋል? / How it spreads
ዴንጊ የሚተላለፈው ቫይረሱን በያዘ ሴት ትንኝ ንክሻ አማካኝነት ነው። ይህ ትንኝ በዋነኝነት **በቀን ብርሃን** ይነክሳል፤

### ምልክቶች / Symptoms
ብዙውን ጊዜ ምልክቶች ከንክሻ በኋላ በ4–10 ቀናት ውስጥ ይጀምራሉ፤ ለ2–7 ቀናትም ይቆያሉ። ሊያካትቱ የሚችሉት፦

**Key facts.** Dengue is a viral infection spread to people through the bite of infected *Aedes*
(mainly *Aedes aegypti*) mosquitoes. It is common in warm, humid areas and is not spread directly
person-to-person. Most infected people have no or mild symptoms and recover in 1–2 weeks.
Occasionally dengue becomes severe — a life-threatening emergency. There is no specific antiviral;
early recognition of warning signs and proper supportive care greatly reduce deaths. The main
prevention is avoiding mosquito bites and removing mosquito breeding sites.

**How it spreads.** Transmitted by the bite of an infected female mosquito that bites mainly during
daylight (peaking early morning and before dusk) and breeds in standing clean water (storage
containers, flower pots, discarded tyres, buckets, lids).

**Symptoms** (usually 4–10 days after the bite, lasting 2–7 days): high fever (up to 39–40°C),
severe headache, pain behind the eyes, muscle/joint/bone pain, nausea, vomiting, rash, swollen
glands.

**Warning signs of severe dengue** (often as fever falls, day 3–7) — seek care immediately: severe
abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding gums/nose, blood in vomit or stool, rapid breathing,
fatigue/restlessness, pale cold skin.

**Prevention and control.** (1) Remove breeding sites: cover, empty and clean water-storage
containers weekly; remove discarded items that hold water; change water in flower pots and plant
saucers; clear blocked drains. (2) Personal protection: wear long-sleeved clothing; use repellents;
fit window/door screens; use insecticide-treated bed nets especially for daytime sleepers (infants,
the sick, the elderly). (3) Community action: clean-up campaigns, proper waste disposal, cooperate
with vector-control spraying.

**Treatment.** No specific cure; supportive care — fluids and rest; use paracetamol for fever/pain;
avoid aspirin and ibuprofen/other NSAIDs (bleeding risk); seek care urgently if warning signs appear.
