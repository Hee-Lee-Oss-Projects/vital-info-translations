## 3. Source text (English) — for reviewer comparison only

*Reconstructed from the WHO "Infant and young child feeding" fact sheet — VERIFY VERBATIM (see Provenance note).*

### Key facts
- WHO and UNICEF recommend: **early initiation of breastfeeding within the first hour of birth**; **exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months of life**; and introduction of nutritionally adequate and safe **complementary (solid) foods at 6 months** together with **continued breastfeeding up to 2 years of age or beyond**.
- **Exclusive breastfeeding** means the infant receives **only breast milk** — no other food or drink, **not even water** — with the exception of oral rehydration solution, or drops or syrups of vitamins, minerals or medicines when these are needed.
- Breast milk is the ideal food for infants. It is safe, clean and contains antibodies that help protect against many common childhood illnesses.
- Breastfeeding protects against **diarrhoea** and common childhood illnesses such as **pneumonia**, and may also have longer-term health benefits.
- Improving breastfeeding could save the lives of many children under 5 years of age each year.

### What "exclusive breastfeeding" means
Exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months means the infant **receives only breast milk** — from the mother or a wet nurse, or expressed breast milk — and **no other liquids or solids**, not even water, with the exception of oral rehydration solution, drops or syrups of vitamins, minerals or medicines.

### Why breastfeeding matters
Breast milk provides all the energy and nutrients that an infant needs for the **first 6 months of life**. It continues to provide up to half or more of a child's nutritional needs during the **second half of the first year**, and up to one third during the **second year of life**.

- Breast milk promotes **healthy growth and brain development**.
- Breastfed children perform better on intelligence tests and are **less likely to be overweight or obese** and less prone to diabetes later in life.
- Women who breastfeed have a **reduced risk of breast and ovarian cancers**.
- Breast milk is **always available and affordable**, which helps to ensure that infants get adequate nutrition.

### Recommendations for mothers and families
- **Start breastfeeding within the first hour after birth.** Place the baby in skin-to-skin contact with the mother.
- Give the newborn **colostrum** — the thick yellowish milk produced in the first days. It is rich in protective factors and is the perfect first food. **Do not** throw it away.
- **Breastfeed exclusively for the first 6 months** — give **no** other foods or drinks, **not even water**, unless medically indicated.
- **Breastfeed on demand** — as often as the child wants, **day and night**, including at least 8 times in 24 hours in the early weeks.
- Let the baby finish one breast before offering the other, so the baby gets the nutrient-rich later milk.
- **Avoid bottles, teats (artificial nipples) and pacifiers (dummies)**, which can interfere with breastfeeding and carry a risk of infection.
- **Continue breastfeeding up to 2 years of age or beyond**, adding adequate and safe complementary foods from **6 months**.
- A mother who is unwell, or whose baby is unwell, should usually **keep breastfeeding**; seek advice from a health worker rather than stopping.

### Support for breastfeeding
Breastfeeding has to be **learned**, and many mothers face difficulties at the start. **Skilled support** — counselling, and help with correct **positioning and attachment** of the baby at the breast — helps mothers breastfeed successfully. Family, community and workplace support (including paid maternity leave and a private space and time to breastfeed or express milk) helps mothers continue exclusive breastfeeding. If you have pain, sore nipples, or worry that the baby is not getting enough milk, seek help from a health worker or breastfeeding counsellor early.

## 4. Hausa translation / Fassara cikin Hausa

*Fassarar WHO "Infant and young child feeding" fact sheet (sashen shayar da nono kaɗai). Asalin rubutu na Turanci ne ke da inganci da iko (binding); duba sashe na 8 don sanarwar fassara.*

### Muhimman bayanai
- WHO da UNICEF sun ba da shawarar: **fara shayar da nono cikin sa'a ɗaya ta farko bayan haihuwa**; **shayar da nono kaɗai a watanni 6 na farko na rayuwa**; da kuma fara ba da **abinci na ƙari (abinci mai ƙarfi) mai gina jiki da aminci tun yana wata 6** tare da **ci gaba da shayar da nono har shekara 2 ko fiye da haka**.
- **Shayar da nono kaɗai** na nufin jariri yana sha **nono kawai** — babu wani abinci ko abin sha, **ko da ruwa ma a'a** — sai dai maganin gyaran ruwan jiki (ORS), ko ɗigogi ko ruwan magani na bitamin, ma'adanai ko magunguna idan akwai buƙata.
- Nonon uwa shi ne abinci mafi kyau ga jariri. Yana da aminci, tsabta, kuma yana ɗauke da **garkuwar jiki (antibodies)** da ke taimakawa wajen kāriya daga yawancin cututtukan ƙuruciya.
- Shayar da nono na bā da kāriya daga **gudawa (diarrhoea)** da cututtukan ƙuruciya da suka zama ruwan dare kamar **ciwon huhu (pneumonia)**, kuma yana iya kawo amfani ga lafiya na dogon lokaci.
- Inganta shayar da nono na iya ceto rayukan yara da yawa 'yan ƙasa da shekara 5 a kowace shekara.

### Ma'anar "shayar da nono kaɗai"
Shayar da nono kaɗai a watanni 6 na farko na nufin jariri yana **sha nono kawai** — daga uwa ko mai shayarwa, ko nonon da aka matse a ajiye — kuma **babu wani abin sha ko abinci mai ƙarfi**, ko da ruwa ma a'a, sai dai maganin gyaran ruwan jiki (ORS), ɗigogi ko ruwan magani na bitamin, ma'adanai ko magunguna.

### Me ya sa shayar da nono yake da muhimmanci
Nonon uwa na bā da dukkan kuzari da abubuwan gina jiki da jariri ke buƙata a **watanni 6 na farko na rayuwa**. Yana ci gaba da bā da rabi ko fiye na buƙatun abinci na yaro a **rabin shekara ta farko na biyu**, da kuma har kashi ɗaya bisa uku a **shekara ta biyu ta rayuwa**.

- Nonon uwa na taimakawa wajen **girma cikin lafiya da bunƙasar ƙwaƙwalwa**.
- Yaran da aka shayar da nono suna yin sakamako mafi kyau a gwajin hankali, kuma **ƙila ba za su yi ƙiba fiye da kima ba** kuma ba sa saurin kamuwa da ciwon suga (diabetes) a nan gaba.
- Matan da suke shayar da nono suna da **ƙarancin haɗarin kamuwa da cutar kansa ta nono da ta ƙwai (ovary)**.
- Nonon uwa **koyaushe yana samuwa kuma ba shi da tsada**, wanda hakan ke taimakawa jariri ya samu isasshen gina jiki.

### Shawarwari ga uwaye da iyalai
- **Fara shayar da nono cikin sa'a ɗaya ta farko bayan haihuwa.** A sa jaririn ya yi mannawa fata-da-fata da uwa (skin-to-skin).
- A bā wa jariri **nonon farko (colostrum)** — nono mai kauri mai launin rawaya da ake samarwa a kwanaki na farko. Yana cike da abubuwan kāriya kuma shi ne abinci na farko mafi kyau. **Kada** a zubar da shi.
- **A shayar da nono kaɗai a watanni 6 na farko** — **kada** a bā da wani abinci ko abin sha, **ko da ruwa ma a'a**, sai dai idan likita ya ce akwai buƙata.
- **A shayar da nono duk lokacin da jaririn yake so** — kullum yana so, **rana da dare**, har sau 8 a cikin sa'o'i 24 a makwannin farko.
- A bar jariri ya gama nono ɗaya kafin a ba shi ɗaya, domin ya samu nonon ƙarshe mai wadataccen gina jiki.
- **A guji kwalabe, da nono na roba (teats), da abin tsotsa (pacifier/dummy)**, waɗanda za su iya tarwatsa shayarwa kuma suna ɗauke da haɗarin kamuwa da cuta.
- **A ci gaba da shayar da nono har shekara 2 ko fiye da haka**, ana ƙara abinci na ƙari mai isa da aminci tun yana wata **6**.
- Uwar da ba ta da lafiya, ko wadda jaririnta ba shi da lafiya, ya kamata galibi ta **ci gaba da shayarwa**; a nemi shawarar ma'aikacin lafiya maimakon dakatarwa.

### Tallafi ga shayar da nono
Ana **koyon** shayar da nono ne, kuma uwaye da yawa suna fuskantar wahala a farko. **Tallafi na ƙwararru** — bayar da shawara, da taimako wajen **daidaita riƙon jariri da haɗa baki da nono yadda ya kamata (positioning da attachment)** — yana taimaka wa uwa ta yi shayarwa cikin nasara. Tallafi daga iyali, al'umma da wurin aiki (haɗe da hutun haihuwa mai biya da wuri da lokaci na sirri don shayarwa ko matse nono) yana taimaka wa uwa ta ci gaba da shayar da nono kaɗai. Idan kina jin zafi, ko nono ya yi ciwo, ko kina damuwa cewa jaririn bai isa nono ba, ki nemi taimako daga ma'aikacin lafiya ko mai ba da shawara kan shayarwa da wuri.
